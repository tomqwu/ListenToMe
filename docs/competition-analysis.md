# Competition Analysis

_Last updated: 2026-06. All pricing and feature facts are stated as of 2026; where a detail could not be confirmed from a primary source, it is qualified with "approximately" or "reportedly."_

## The category: AI meeting assistants and real-time copilots

Over the last few years, "AI meeting assistant" has grown from a niche transcription utility into a crowded product category. The tools in this space cluster into a few overlapping shapes:

- **Bot-joining note-takers** that send a visible recording bot into Zoom / Google Meet / Microsoft Teams calls, then produce transcripts, summaries, and action items afterward (Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai, Fathom, Fellow, tl;dv).
- **Bot-free desktop capturers** that listen to your computer's own microphone and system audio locally and augment your notes without joining the call as a participant (Granola, Superpowered).
- **Real-time copilots / interview-assist overlays** that surface live answer suggestions during a call, sometimes explicitly marketed as "undetectable" (Cluely and a wider category of interview-assist tools).
- **On-device transcription tools** that run speech-to-text fully on the local machine for privacy-sensitive users (MacWhisper, and the open-source Natively).

Two structural tensions define the category. First, **privacy vs. convenience**: nearly every commercial product processes audio and runs its AI in the cloud, even when it markets itself as "local-first" — the local part is usually just audio _capture_, while transcription and summarization happen on third-party servers. Second, **opinionated vs. open**: most products lock you into a single (often undisclosed) transcription engine and a single summarization LLM with no user choice, whereas a small set of privacy-first / open tools let you bring your own model.

**ListenToMe** sits deliberately at the intersection of the privacy-first and copilot shapes: a fully on-device macOS meeting copilot that transcribes live with Apple's SpeechAnalyzer and gives real-time AI help through Ollama (local and cloud models), with per-pane model selection, language selection, use-case presets, file/folder context, and Markdown export — free and open-source.

## Per-competitor detail

### Granola
Granola is a bot-free "AI notepad for back-to-back meetings" running natively on macOS, Windows, and iOS that captures your computer's own system audio and mic rather than joining calls as a visible bot, so it works across Zoom, Meet, Teams, and Webex. Despite "local-first" marketing, audio is streamed to third-party cloud ASR (it names Deepgram and AssemblyAI) and summarization runs on OpenAI/Anthropic models in the cloud; raw audio is discarded after transcription and text is stored encrypted in US AWS, with de-identified self-training offered on an opt-out basis. Pricing as of 2026 is approximately a free Basic tier, Business ~$14/user/mo, and Enterprise ~$35/user/mo. Source: <https://granola.ai/security>

### Otter.ai
Otter is a cloud-based assistant for web, iOS, Android, desktop, and Chrome whose "OtterPilot" bot auto-joins Zoom/Teams/Meet for real-time transcription, summaries, action items, and Otter AI Chat, and in 2026 it repositioned as a "Conversational Knowledge Engine" with an MCP server. It uses its own proprietary ASR (explicitly not Whisper) and leans on Anthropic's Claude for summarization; notably, its privacy policy permits training proprietary models on de-identified audio and transcriptions that "may contain Personal Information," which sits in tension with its no-training marketing. Pricing as of 2026 is approximately a free tier (300 min/mo), Pro ~$8.33–16.99/user/mo, and Business ~$20–30/user/mo. Source: <https://otter.ai/privacy-security>

### Fireflies.ai
Fireflies is a cloud-based assistant (web, macOS/Windows desktop, mobile, Chrome extension) whose bot records and transcribes Zoom/Teams/Meet/Webex, processing and storing data in its US cloud (AWS + GCP) with a zero-day vendor-retention policy and a stated no-training-on-customer-content position. It is reportedly Whisper-based for transcription (third-party attribution, not officially confirmed) with its own AI layer for summaries, the AskFred chat assistant, and the Live Assist real-time copilot added in late 2025. Pricing as of 2026 is approximately free, Pro ~$10–18/seat/mo, Business ~$19–29, and Enterprise ~$39. Source: <https://fireflies.ai/security>

### Fathom
Fathom is a cloud-based AI meeting assistant for Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams with a desktop app and a 2026 iOS app, storing data in US/Canada AWS and holding SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA, and GDPR with AI sub-processors contractually barred from training on customer data. A 2026 update added bot-free capture, account-wide insights, and live summaries; "bring your own LLM" is delivered via its public API/MCP integrations rather than an in-app model picker. Pricing as of 2026 runs from a free tier through Business at $34/user/mo, with a general-meeting focus that leans toward sales/conversation intelligence. Source: <https://www.fathom.ai/pricing>

### Fellow
Fellow began as a meeting-agenda/productivity tool and is now an AI-first meeting assistant (Fellow.ai), notable for being the most cross-platform of the bot-joiners — macOS/Windows desktop, iOS/Android, and Chrome/Firefox extensions, with both bot and botless capture across Meet/Zoom/Teams/Slack. It has a strong privacy posture (SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA, GDPR, CCPA; AWS Canada; a "Zero-Day Retention" option) and, per third-party reporting, routes its AI layer through AssemblyAI's LLM Gateway with no user-facing model picker. Pricing as of 2026 runs approximately Free → Team ~$7 → Business ~$15 → Enterprise ~$25/user/mo. Source: <https://help.fellow.ai/en/articles/4302231-security-and-compliance>

### tl;dv
tl;dv is a cloud-based assistant for Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams positioned primarily around sales/revenue teams (CRM sync, sales coaching, MEDDIC/BANT/SPIN scoring), with EU-based data storage and SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR, and EU AI Act compliance. It uses OpenAI Whisper (via Google Cloud) for transcription and chunks/anonymizes meetings before sending them to Anthropic for summaries, with no user-selectable LLMs. Pricing as of 2026 runs from a generous free tier through Pro ~$18/user/mo and Business ~$59/user/mo (annual) to custom Enterprise. Source: <https://tldv.io/features/security-commitment/>

### Cluely
Cluely is a real-time, screen-overlay AI assistant for live calls (interviews, sales, exams) that listens, transcribes, and surfaces hotkey-triggered answer suggestions, with a premium "Undetectability" mode that renders the overlay via low-level GPU hooks so it stays hidden from screen sharing. As of 2026 it offers a macOS desktop app and an iOS app; it is cloud-based, does not disclose which LLMs it uses, and does not allow model choice. It is explicitly not privacy-first and reportedly suffered a 2025 data breach exposing 80,000+ users; pricing is approximately a free Starter, Pro $19.99/mo, and Pro + Undetectability $149.99/mo. Source: <https://cluely.com/pricing>

### Interview-assist tools (category)
A crowded 2026 category of real-time AI overlays designed to feed live answers during interviews while evading screen-capture detection, including Final Round AI (~$149/mo, supports coding rounds), Interview Coder (coding-interview focused), LockedIn AI (~$54.99–119.99/mo plus a stealth mode), and others such as Interview Sidekick, Parakeet AI, and Verve AI. The common technique is system-level/GPU rendering that bypasses the screen-capture layer; transcription engines and LLMs are generally undisclosed and model choice is rarely offered. These tools share ListenToMe's "real-time copilot" shape but are narrowly aimed at interviews and are cloud-based. Source: <https://www.finalroundai.com/blog/best-undetectable-ai-interview-tools>

### Natively (open-source)
Natively is an open-source, free, local-first alternative to Cluely/Otter/Granola, doing on-device speech-to-text (ONNX Moonshine + Whisper-large-v3-turbo, accelerated via CoreML/Metal or DirectML) and storing transcripts, embeddings, and keys locally by default while letting users plug in cloud LLMs. It runs on macOS 12+ and Windows 10/11 (with a community Linux build), offers a real-time copilot, summaries, action items, local RAG/semantic search, diarization, and personas, and has a strong BYOK/multi-model story (Gemini, OpenAI, Claude, Groq, and local Ollama). Pricing as of 2026 is free for personal/non-commercial use with a Pro lifetime/yearly tier; it is the closest direct philosophical competitor to ListenToMe, though more interview-focused and cross-platform rather than macOS-native. Source: <https://github.com/Natively-AI-assistant/natively-cluely-ai-assistant>

### Superpowered
Superpowered is a bot-free AI meeting notetaker for macOS and Windows that captures device audio live and generates structured AI notes via templates, emphasizing a no-recordings privacy posture: audio is deleted immediately after live transcription and transcripts are kept only ~7 days, with SOC 2 Type 2 and GDPR compliance. Its transcription engine is not officially specified (third-party reports cite Deepgram), and although AI processing is cloud-based, it advertises no multi-model or BYO-model support. Pricing as of 2026 is approximately a free tier (10 AI notes/mo), Basic $25/mo, and Pro $50/mo. Source: <https://superpowered.me/>

### MacWhisper
MacWhisper is a polished Mac transcription app built on OpenAI Whisper that runs inference fully on-device — audio never leaves the machine by default — making it popular with journalists, lawyers, and therapists. It primarily transcribes audio/video files, URLs, and meetings (with real-time/system-audio capture as a secondary feature) and offers optional AI post-processing (cleanup, summaries, action items, chat) via your own API keys (OpenAI, Anthropic/Claude, Google, Groq in the Gumroad version) or a paid cloud Assistant subscription. Pricing as of 2026 is approximately a free tier, a Gumroad Pro at ~€59/$69 one-time, and an App Store version at $6.99/mo to $99.99 lifetime. Source: <https://goodsnooze.gumroad.com/l/macwhisper>

## Positioning for ListenToMe

ListenToMe occupies a corner of the market that almost no commercial competitor genuinely fills: a **fully on-device, real-time meeting copilot** that is also **free and open-source**. The differentiators stack up against the field as follows:

- **Fully on-device and private.** Transcription runs locally via Apple SpeechAnalyzer, and AI help can run entirely against a local Ollama model — so audio and transcript need never leave the Mac. Granola, Otter, Fireflies, Fathom, Fellow, tl;dv, Cluely, and Superpowered all process audio and/or run their AI in the cloud, even when marketed as "local-first." Only MacWhisper and the open-source Natively share this on-device foundation.
- **Bring-your-own-model (Ollama local + cloud).** ListenToMe lets users choose their own models — local via Ollama or cloud — rather than being locked to a single undisclosed backend LLM. Most competitors (Otter, Fireflies, Fathom, Fellow, tl;dv, Cluely, Superpowered) offer no user-facing model choice at all; only MacWhisper and Natively are comparable here.
- **Multi-pane, multi-model.** Per-pane model selection lets a user run different models side by side for the same conversation (for example a fast local model for live notes and a stronger model for analysis). No mainstream competitor exposes this multi-pane / multi-model workflow.
- **Multi-purpose via use-case presets.** Rather than being pinned to a single vertical (sales for tl;dv, interviews for Cluely, file transcription for MacWhisper), use-case presets plus file/folder context let one app serve meetings, interviews, study, support, and more.
- **Free and open-source.** No subscription, no seat pricing, and the code is open for inspection — a sharp contrast to the $14–149/mo commercial tiers and the closed, undisclosed pipelines of most of the category.

**One-line positioning:** _ListenToMe is the free, open-source, fully on-device meeting copilot for macOS — bring your own model, run it private, and shape it to any conversation._

## Gaps / opportunities

These are **future ideas, not commitments** — areas where competitors are currently ahead and where ListenToMe could close the gap while staying true to its on-device, privacy-first principles.

1. **Calendar integration / auto-launch.** Granola, Fathom, Fellow, and Superpowered tie into the user's calendar to auto-start on scheduled meetings and pre-load context. A privacy-respecting local calendar hook (e.g. EventKit on macOS) could offer the same convenience without sending data anywhere.
2. **Speaker diarization.** Otter, Fireflies, and Natively label who said what. On-device diarization (locally, without cloud) would meaningfully improve transcript usefulness for multi-person meetings.
3. **Shareable summaries / structured exports.** Competitors generate polished, templated summaries and shareable links. ListenToMe already does Markdown export; richer summary templates and optional export targets (PDF, clipboard-ready recap, task lists) would help, ideally all generated locally.
4. **Cross-meeting search / local knowledge base.** AskFred (Fireflies), Ask Granola, and Natively's local RAG let users query across past meetings. A fully on-device semantic index over past sessions would extend this without compromising privacy.
5. **Templates / presets library for action items and follow-ups.** Many tools ship dozens of meeting templates (1-1s, sales discovery, interviews). Expanding the use-case preset library — community-contributed, since the project is open-source — could broaden reach.
6. **Mobile / companion presence.** Several competitors offer iOS/Android companions. A lightweight companion or live-handoff experience could extend ListenToMe beyond the desktop while keeping processing on-device where possible.
